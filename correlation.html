The Balance Sheet: Leverage and Default​

The balance sheet for a person or a company shows their financial position at a specific point in time. It consists of three main components: assets, liabilities, and equity, the relation is given by the fundemental accounting equation:

Assets=Liabilities+EquityAssets = Liabilities + Equity Assets=Liabilities+Equity

Where:

  • Assets: e.g. cash, house, car, investments, etc.
  • Liabilities: e.g. mortgage, loans, credit card debt, etc.
  • Equity: Assets - Liabilities / net worth / owner's equity.

Let's say I have 150k before buying a house, and I buy a house for 500k with a mortgage of 400k. Before, I have cash only.

  • Assets: 150k(Cash)
  • Liabilities: 0
  • Equity: 150k

After:

I have a house worth 500k, a mortgage of 400k, and cash of 150k - 100k = 50k.

  • Assets: 500k(House) + 50k(Cash) = 550k
  • Liabilities: 400k(Mortgage)
  • Equity: 550k - 400k = 150k

Measuring Leverage with Equity Multiplier​

The equity multiplier (EM) is a measure of financial leverage, calculated as:

The Housing Finance Problem and its Solutions​

Housing Microfinance (HMF)

  • Provides small loans to low-income households or individuals who lack access to traditional banking services, enabling them to improve their housing conditions.

  • What are the main driver of market growth of HMF?

    • Rising urbanization and housing demand in developing countries.
    • Increasing awareness about HMF services.
    • Supportive government policies and initiatives.

  • What are the main constraints to market growth of HMF?

    • Limited access to funding for HMF providers.
    • High operational costs and risks associated with lending to low-income borrowers.
    • Lack of awareness and understanding of HMF products among potential clients.

Contracual savings for housing (CSH)

  • Financial institutions that collect regular savings from individuals and provide them with affordable housing loans.

  • Difference between open and closed CSH?

    • Open CSH: allow members to join and leave freely, providing flexibility.
    • Closed CSH: have a fixed membership, often tied to specific organizations or communities.

  • How CSH minimize credit risk?

    • Creditworthiness of members before granting loans.
    • Strict repayment schedules and monitoring mechanisms.
    • Encouraging savings habits among members.

Housing bonds

  • Debt securities issued by governments or financial institutions to raise funds for housing projects.

Mortgage liquidity facilities (MLF)

  • Financial mechanisms that provide liquidity to mortgage lenders, enabling them to offer more loans to homebuyers.

REITs vs REOCs​

Both REITs (Real Estate Investment Trusts) and REOCs (Real Estate Operating Companies):

  • Primarily own residential real estate.
  • Derive most of their income from rent or property sale.
  • Are publicly listed on an exchange.
  • Eliminate the hassle of actually owning, letting, and managing properties yourself.
  • Allow even small investors to buy a slice of the income generated by a number of different properties.

The main difference is:

REITsREOCs
Distribute at least 90% of taxable income as dividends to shareholdersCan retain earnings for growth and development
Benefit from special tax treatment, avoiding corporate income taxSubject to corporate income tax on profits
Shareholders pay taxes on dividends receivedShareholders pay taxes on dividends and capital gains
